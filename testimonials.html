What do you call the pointy end?

In 2014, my husband and I bought a Catalina 445 sailing vessel. Exciting, except I had never sailed and didn't know the first thing about boats. However, I was determined to live the dream, so I looked for a captain who could teach me, well, everything. After a couple of false and discouraging starts with other captains, I was referred to Captain Z and I knew I had finally found someone who could teach me what I needed to know to become a sailor.

As I mentioned, I had a couple of false starts. What sold me initially on Capt. Z was that he operated with integrity. I immediately understood that I could trust him to take care that I didn't hurt myself or my boat. He quickly demonstrated boat handling skills that made me trust that he could get me out of whatever impending catastrophe I managed to get myself into! He was always well prepared and had a plan but would make adjustments appropriately, according to weather and wind. He taught by example to respect the sea and its conditions so sailing was FUN!

Throughout my experience, I have been impressed by the depth and breadth of the Captain's knowledge and his passion for life on the water. He shares his knowledge in a clear and concise manner with an intuitive understanding of how a person learns so each lesson builds both skills and confidence.

And the results? We started with the basics (the pointy end is the bow, by the way). I worked with the Captain for a solid month on docking, sailing and general boat knowledge. He taught me so well that I was able to challenge the ASA tests 101 and 103 and pass with flying colors. I also learned to dock my boat well enough for people watching to comment on my skill and I can plot a course using both paper and electronic methods. I am also capable of performing all preventive maintenance and understand all the systems on the boat.

To say Captain Z has had a huge impact on my sailing life is an understatement. The fact that I feel confident in my boat handling skills has enhanced both my husband's and my own enjoyment of our time spent on the water and we look forward to a life full of cruising adventures. And for that, I have to thank Captain Z.

Karen Posada

Bliss Point
